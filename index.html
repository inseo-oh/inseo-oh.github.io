Human language skills

Korean

My native language, though I no longer use it on my channel and social platforms, mainly because I got tired of doing translations.

English

I learned English from acadamy when I was child, and I also probably watched millions of English videos(and I still do) on YouTube. So I think my English skills are okay, at least for what I do.
Currently my limitations are:

  • My brain's English dictionary is mostly built for technology. In other words, I am not good at words you actually use in real life.
  • If someone speaks fast, I can't understand very well.
  • I'm not confident enough to speak in English, though I can write English just fine.

So when I open takling live stream from one of Hololive EN members for example, I usually give up watching after 1 minute.

Knowing English was(and is) very helpful for computer adventures, not just because I can easily read the program code(unless one uses horrifying naming convention), but also I can read reference documentation written in English without any help(in most cases).

I'm also glad that I have OK English skills, because it's pretty tough to find Korean people with similar interests to mine, since I've gone too deep into computing. Almost nobody near me would undestand what I'm talking about.

Japanese

Almost non-existent. I'm slowly learning Japanese, and I can in fact read most Hiragana, and around 60% of Katakana I think. But of course I don't know a lot of Japanese words, and Kanji is of course the biggest hurdle.

Me: Oh yeah now I remember what it was.

I've gone too deep into computing

I'm pretty sure that I'm one of those people who loves computer too much and have fallen into multiple very in-depth topics. Here's some of what I've done over the years:

  • Watching videos about computer security, especially ones for video game consoles. It's interesting to see how game consoles prevent users from running their own software, and also learn how it was broken. And enjoy some hilarious moments like how Sony managed to "leak" their signing private key thanks to their laughable ECDSA implementation.
  • Watching videos about vintage UNIX machines(Sun, IBM RS/6000, etc...).
  • Writing hardware emulation code, though I've never finished any of them. I wrote 6502 and MC68000 emulators in the past, and had partial success with writing my own Apple I and II, C64 emulator. I tried writing Atari ST emulator with goal being booting to EmuTOS desktop, but it never booted properly.
  • Try to make my own OS. I don't do this anymore, as I learned that I can't handle big projects on myself. And writing an OS is very big project.
    But during the process, I learned how to write x86-64 assembly, basic concepts about virtual addressing, how swap memory(or Paging File under Windows) can be implemented, some details about x86 and x86-64 architecture. And I did write an incredibly bare-bones read-only ext2 filesystem driver, preemptive multitasking microkernel, IPC system for two processes to communicate, my own graphics drawing routines, etc...

Things that I don't do anymore

Sometimes I start doing something, and then realize how bad I'm at it many, many years later.

  • Live streaming: I used to do live streaming on Twitch for many years, but without any meaningful result. Later, after months of break, I decided to give another shot.
    But while I think I became better at streaming than before, it didn't took too long to realize I didn't feel comfortable at continuous talking on live. And there's almost nothing I could do other than talking. I don't play video games, cooking, or anything else really.
    Eventually, I stopped doing live streaming, and decided to focus on studying frontend web development.
    I did try some programming on stream, but not only no existing viewers were interested, but also I just didn't feel comfortable when writing code on live.
    The software I used for streaming is of course Open Broadcaster Software.
  • Drawing illusts: I was also drawing illusts because it necessary when I was doing live streaming. I don't ask somebody else to make assets for my contents. If I don't do it myself, I don't feel like it's purely my content.
    However, while I was clearly getting better than years ago, I think it's still comically bad for any serious usage. Eventually, not only I stopped drawing things entirely, ALL of my existing music box videos were recreated with new design with original album art, using the C program I mentioned above.
